Kool Lady Blue is one of the most important entrepreneur, promoter and manager in the hiphop history. She introduced hiphop to the world south of 125th St. and the South Bronx, at her legendary venues Negril and Wheels Of Steel at the Roxy. THE "ROXY ROLLER RINK" ON 18TH ST. was transformed into one if the greatest hiphop clubs of all times, with legends like the Rock Steady, Double Trouble, Cold Crush 4, Fantastic 5, Buzy Bee, Starskie and many more. In 1982 she became the manager of the Rock Steady Crew (who were founded in 1977) at a time when the crew`s popularity escalated. She booked them on the Roxy Tour "The NYC Rap Tour", together with Afrika Bambaataa, Phase 2,The Double Dutch Girls, Grand Mixer DST and the infinity Rappers, Fab 5 Freddy, Rammellzee to London and Paris. It was the first tour of this kind mixing DJ's, MC`s and graffiti artists.

Now Kool Lady Blue is still in the biz, she`s booking Afrika Bambaataa & Grandmaster Flash exclusively! She keeps them busy traveling the world. Perhaps some of you have seen them on the Germany tour this year.

Ricky Powell, aka the Rickster toured for two decades with the biggest hiphop acts or spotted everyday people on the street, only equiped with an instamatic camera. In "Public Access: Ricky Powell Photographs 1985–2005", Powell presents the actors, musicians, performers, and artists that inspired him, including Method Man, Doize Green, Bill Adler, Slick Rick, Run DMC, Jean-Michel Basquiat, Eric B. and Rakim, Keith Haring, Public Enemy, Sophia Coppola, Nina Hagen, Biz Markie, LL Cool J and many more. Also presented in Public Access are Powell’s never-before-published collaborations with graffiti writers and photographers including Ron Galella, Ron English, Phil Frost, Lee Quinones, Sam Flores, David Choe, Dalek, Dr. Revolt, Zephyr, Quik, Team, Daze, Seen, Kaws, Jest, Bigfoot, Ssur, and Haze, among others.
